ALOE

(Aloe vera, Aloe perfoliata, Aloe arborescens natalenis, Aloe ferox,Aloe africana, Aloe spicata)

GENERAL INFORMATION:

The use of herbs, vitamins, minerals and other dietary supplements is becomingvery popular. As more people use dietary supplements, more interactionsare reported. Some of these interactions may be very harmful; others mayonly be reported in animals or in the laboratory at this time.

Keep in mind that this document may not include every interaction thatis known. Although much research has been done to cover as many interactionsas possible, you should still talk to your healthcare provider before takingdietary supplements. Even if a medicine, supplement, or food is not listedbelow, an interaction is still possible. This is also true for interactionswith alcohol and tobacco.

This information is an educational aid only. It is not intended as medicaladvice for individual conditions or treatments. Talk to your healthcareprovider before following any medical regimen to see if it is safe andeffective for you.

This document contains interaction information only. If you would likeother information about this supplement, such as correct dose or side effects,talk to your healthcare provider.

INTERACTIONS WITH:

PRESCRIPTION MEDICINE:

The following medicine used to thin the blood and prevent blood clots (anticoagulant):

warfarin (Coumadin(R))

HARMFUL EFFECT:

Using aloe gel and warfarin may cause you to bleed more easily.

PROOF:

This interaction has not been studied and is not proven.  Based upon the way that aloe interacts with other medicines, it is thought that this interaction may be possible (3).

WHAT TO DO:

Using aloe gel together with warfarin may cause an increased risk of certain side effects. If you are using both medicines together, your doctor may change the dose or how often you use one or both of the medicines. It is important to tell your doctor and pharmacist about all other medicines that you are using. Call your doctor if you have blood in your urine; have black, bloody, or sticky bowel movements; have blood in your vomit; have a nosebleed that lasts longer than 15 minutes; have new or unusual bruising; feel weak or dizzy. Do not stop using your medicines without talking to your doctor first.

The following medicine used to treat heart failure and certain heart rhythm problems (also called digitalis):

digoxin (Lanoxicaps(R), Lanoxin(R))

HARMFUL EFFECT:

Taking aloe and digoxin may cause the amount of digoxin in your blood to be too high and you may have more side effects.

PROOF:

This interaction has not been studied and is not proven, but it is possiblethat it may occur based on the information available aboutherbs and drugs (1).

WHAT TO DO:

Using aloe together with digoxin may cause an increased risk of certain sideeffects, but using both drugs may be the best treatment for you. If you areusing both medicines together, your doctor may change the dose or how oftenyou use one or both of the medicines. It is important to tell your doctorand pharmacist about all other medicines that you are using. Call your doctorif you have chest pain or discomfort, have unusual heartbeats, feel dizzy,feel weak, feel lightheaded, or have nausea and vomiting. Do not stop usingyour medicines without talking to your doctor first.

The following medicine used to make you lose consciousness (fall asleep) before having surgery (called anesthesia):

sevoflurane (Ultane(R))

HARMFUL EFFECT:

Taking aloe vera and receiving sevoflurane during anesthesia may increase the risk of bleeding.

PROOF:

This interaction has not been studied and is not proven, but it is possible that it may occur based on the information available about drugs and herbs (2).

WHAT TO DO:

Let your doctor know if you are taking herbal supplements that contain aloe vera before your surgery. Avoid taking aloe vera at least one week before surgery.

NONPRESCRIPTION (OVER-THE-COUNTER)MEDICINE:

At the time this document was written, no interactions between aloe and nonprescription (over-the-counter) medicine were found.

DIETARY SUPPLEMENTS:

At the time this document was written, no interactions between aloe and dietary supplements were found.

FOOD:

At the time this document was written, no interactions between aloe and food were found.

ALCOHOL:

At the time this document was written, no interactions between aloe and alcohol were found.

TOBACCO:

At the time this document was written, no interactions between aloe and tobacco were found.
