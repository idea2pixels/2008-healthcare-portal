Each time your heart beats, it pumps blood through your arteries. This creates pressure in them. Your blood pressure consists of two numbers. The first or upper number is your systolic blood pressure. It represents the pressure in your arteries while your heart is beating. The second or lower number is your diastolic blood pressure. It represents the pressure in your heart is resting between beats. Your blood pressure can change minute to minute, for example, when you change your posture or when you exercise. If your first measure of systolic or diastolic blood pressure is high, your doctor may take several readings before deciding whether your blood pressure is a health concern.
